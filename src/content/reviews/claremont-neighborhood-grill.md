The La Verdita — tequila, cucumber, mint, pineapple, jalapeño, tajin rim — is worth the trip on its own. Perfectly balanced, complex without being fussy, the kind of cocktail that sets unfair expectations for everything that follows.

The room on Northwest Highway is warm and the service legitimately excellent. Our server was personable, funny, and attentive all night — not a given in Dallas, and it made a real difference. If the kitchen matched the front of house, this place would be easy to recommend.

It doesn't. The corned beef bites are tasty and well-proportioned, but they're dense enough to land like a main — go light on them if you're planning a full meal. The crab cake was the clearest miss: flat and unmemorable against the best versions in town right now. The tagliatelle bolognese recovers some ground — the ricotta dollop is a smart call and the dish is honest and solid, just not something to rush back for. The fudge brownie closes strong, as a properly built brownie reliably does.

The math here is odd: drinks and service running at a 9–10, the kitchen running at a 4. That gap makes Claremont better as a cocktail stop where food is secondary than as a full dinner destination. We'd come back for the bar program. We're not steering anyone here for a meal.
