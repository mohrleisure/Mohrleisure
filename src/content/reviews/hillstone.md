Hillstone is one of those restaurants that earns its reputation by doing the basics at an exceptionally high level, every single time. The service here isn't just good — it's consistently elite. Attentive without hovering, warm without being performative, and knowledgeable across the full menu and drink list. It's the standard most restaurants aim for and few actually hit.

Start with The Crush. Belvedere vodka, grapple, fresh mint — it's a 10/10 cocktail, and the fact that it looks as good as it tastes doesn't hurt. It's refreshing, well-balanced, and exactly what a signature cocktail should be: something you order again before the first one is done. The cocktail menu has range beyond it (the Crisp Martini and Signature Margarita are both solid), but The Crush is the reason to make it your first order.

The spinach artichoke dip is the right starter — rich, properly seasoned, served with chips, salsa, and sour cream on the Hillstone branded platter you'll recognize if you've been before. It sets the tone: familiar execution, no corners cut.

For mains, the French dip is the move if you want something in that lane — thick-cut meat, a proper au jus for dipping, coleslaw on the side. The steak with a loaded baked potato covers the classic steakhouse angle and delivers exactly what it promises. Neither dish reinvents anything; both are simply very good versions of what they are.

Dessert: the pecan cobbler with vanilla ice cream is a strong close. Warm, textured, not cloyingly sweet.

**The verdict:** Hillstone is the reliable best option in its category — go for The Crush, stay for dinner, leave satisfied every time.
