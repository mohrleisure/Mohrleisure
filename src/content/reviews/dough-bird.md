Dough Bird is a Fox Restaurant Concepts property — same group behind Flower Child and North Italia — and it reads like one: a polished, busy room at Inwood Village that handles a crowd without falling apart. The concept is exactly what the name says: pizza and rotisserie chicken under one roof, executed at a level that earns a return visit.

The room is dark-industrial with leather booths and pendant lighting on the dining side, and a bar area with checkered floors and green tufted stools that runs a different energy — louder, more social, a solid place to sit if you want to drink your way through the menu first. The space fills up fast on weekends. Reservations make sense.

Start with the cocktail menu. The Spicy Watermelon Margarita is the clear standout — blanco tequila, habanero-watermelon, jalapeño, tajín rim, built for the Texas summer. The cocktail program has range past it (the Texas Colada and a few highball-style options are worth a look), but the Spicy Watermelon is the one to order first.

The potsticker appetizer is the right way to open the table: pan-fried, properly crisp on the bottom, garnished with cilantro and red chili, with a sesame-soy dipping sauce that hits correctly. Light enough to not bury what follows.

The chicken tenders are the signature, and they hold up to the reputation. Crispy, well-seasoned, served with coleslaw and your choice of sauce — the Copy Cat sauce is the call. These are the kind of tenders that make a menu item a reason to come back, not just an afterthought between apps and entrées.

The pizza is serious. The prosciutto and arugula version — thin, properly blistered crust, goat cheese, caramelized onion, fresh arugula piled on top — is the move if you're going the pizza route. The dough is hand-stretched and house-made, which shows in the texture. Shared between two it's the right size; ordered solo it's a full meal.

The rotisserie chicken — which is literally half the concept — arrives with mashed potatoes, gravy, and broccolini, and it's consistently good: juicy bird, well-seasoned skin, nothing fussy. It's the safe reliable order if the pizza and tenders don't grab you.

Save room for the peanut butter brookie. It's the right dessert close — dense, warm, not overly sweet — and it's worth the space.

**The verdict:** Dough Bird earns its reputation without leaning on hype. Good room, strong cocktails, genuinely excellent chicken tenders, and pizza that actually justifies the name being on the marquee. A reliable Inwood Village anchor.
