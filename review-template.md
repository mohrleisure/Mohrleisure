  HOW TO WRITE A MOHR LEISURE REVIEW
  - Voice: an opinionated, well-fed friend telling you about a place. Not a press
    release, not Wikipedia. First-person is welcome ("we split the…").
  - 3–6 sentences is plenty. Lead with the experience, not the history.
  - BANNED words: must-try, hidden gem, iconic, legendary, world-class, delicious,
    amazing, best-kept secret. Say what's specifically good and why instead.
  - Every fact must be true and traceable (your visit, the venue's menu/site).
    Don't invent dishes, prices, awards, or chef names. Unsure? Leave it out.
  - Name the standout dishes/drinks specifically — that's what readers remember.
-->

Open with the moment — the dish or drink that defined the visit.

Then the room and the vibe: what kind of night/meal is this good for, who's it
for, what's the service like.

Close with the verdict — would you go back, and when. Keep it honest; a small
critique makes the praise land harder.
